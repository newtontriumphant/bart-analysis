01 - History & Context

Post-War Uprising and the Transit Crisis

In the late 1940s and 1950s, the Bay Area was experiencing one of the largest suburban migrations in U.S. history. Families ran into new tract-home suburbs and were pretty much entirely dependent on cars. The Bay Bridge was gridlocked daily... something had to give.

By 1950, the Bay Area's population had passed 2.6 million, and it was projected to double in just the next few years. For this reason, in 1951, a Joint Army-Navy Board first proposed a trains-bay railway tube system. The California Legislature approved the BART Commission in 1957. Originally, all five Bay Area counties were supposed to be included, but after years of political issues, the original vision was narrowed down to only three counties - Alameda, Contra Costa, and San Francisco. In 1962, voters were asked to fund it... ...that went over well.
